# SuperSeller AI — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Identity

**Company**: SuperSeller AI
**Tagline**: "Turning Customers into Super Sellers"
**Hero Line**: "Stop Hustling. Start Selling. Your AI Crew Handles the Rest."
**Primary CTA**: "Become a Super Seller"
**Website**: https://superseller.agency
**Legal Entity**: Rensto LLC (DBA SuperSeller Agency)

## Visual System

### Colors
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Orange** | #f47920 | Primary CTAs, highlights, energy, brand signature |
| **Teal** | #4ecdc4 | Secondary actions, success states, data visualizations, accents |
| **Navy** | #0d1b2e | Dark backgrounds, hero sections, professionalism, contrast |

- Orange is the dominant brand color. Every page should have orange present.
- Teal is the complement. Use for secondary elements, never competing with orange for attention.
- Navy is the foundation. Dark sections, footers, hero backgrounds.
- White (#ffffff) and light gray (#f8f9fa) for content areas and cards.
- Never use random blues, reds, or greens outside this palette.

### Typography
- **Primary Font**: Outfit (Google Fonts) -- all headings and body text
- **Subset**: Latin. For non-Latin scripts (Hebrew, Farsi, Hindi, Korean), fall back to Noto Sans with appropriate subset.
- **Headings**: Outfit SemiBold (600) or Bold (700)
- **Body**: Outfit Regular (400)
- **Small/Caption**: Outfit Light (300)

### Logo
- Orange wordmark on dark backgrounds
- Navy wordmark on light backgrounds
- Always include the "AI" suffix: "SuperSeller AI", never just "SuperSeller"

## Voice & Tone

### Who We Sound Like
A founder talking to a fellow business owner over coffee. Direct, practical, a little intense. We built this because we needed it ourselves. Israeli-American entrepreneurial energy -- urgent, no-nonsense, resourceful.

### Voice Attributes
- **Confident**: We know what we built works. No hedging, no "might help", no "could potentially".
- **Direct**: Short sentences. Get to the point. Respect the reader's time.
- **Practical**: Every piece of content must have a takeaway or action. No fluff pieces.
- **Peer-to-peer**: We talk with business owners, not at them. "You" and "your business" -- never "users" or "clients" or "customers" (when referring to our audience).
- **Bold**: It is OK to be provocative. "Your competitors are already using AI. Why aren't you?" works. "Consider exploring AI options" does not.

### Language Rules
**DO**:
- Use "you" and "your business"
- Use "we" to mean the SuperSeller crew
- Lead with outcomes: "Get 10x more listings seen" not "Our platform enables increased visibility"
- Use active voice: "We built" not "It was built"
- Use contractions: "don't", "we're", "you'll"
- Sprinkle Hebrew/Yiddish for personality when appropriate: chutzpah, balagan, yalla, mazel
- Use numbers and specifics: "7 AI agents", "$79/month", "25+ videos"

**DO NOT**:
- Use corporate jargon: "leverage", "synergy", "ecosystem", "holistic", "paradigm", "solutions"
- Use passive voice
- Use generic SaaS language: "powerful platform", "robust solution", "comprehensive suite"
- Fabricate testimonials, case studies, or quotes
- Use emoji walls (1-2 per social post max, zero in formal content)
- Say "users" or "clients" when addressing the audience
- Reference "Rensto" (old brand name) or "Stripe" (migrated to PayPal)

### Tone by Context
| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Social media | Energetic, punchy, conversational |
| Blog posts | Authoritative, educational, still conversational |
| Website pages | Confident, clear, benefit-driven |
| Email | Direct, personal, action-oriented |
| Error messages | Honest, helpful, no blame |
| WhatsApp messages | Casual, fast, emoji-light |

## The AI Crew

SuperSeller AI personifies its products as a "crew" of AI agents. Each has a name, role, and personality. Always reference the correct crew member when discussing a specific product function.

| Crew Member | Role | Product | Personality |

| **Forge** | AI Video Producer | TourReel | Creative, cinematic, detail-obsessed |
| **Spoke** | AI Avatar & Presenter | Avatar System | Charismatic, polished, camera-ready |
| **Market** | AI Marketplace Manager | FB Marketplace Bot | Hustle-oriented, always listing, never sleeps |
| **FrontDesk** | AI Voice Receptionist | Voice AI | Warm, professional, multilingual |
| **Scout** | AI Lead Hunter | Lead Generation | Sharp, data-driven, relentless |
| **Buzz** | AI Social Media Manager | SocialHub | Trendy, engaging, always posting |
| **Cortex** | AI Brain & Strategy | Intelligence Layer | Analytical, strategic, sees the big picture |

### Crew Reference Rules
- When talking about a specific function, use the crew member name: "Forge creates your listing videos" not "Our video tool creates videos."
- Crew members are referred to as "they" (not "it"). They have personality.
- In social posts, introduce crew members: "Meet Forge, your AI video producer."
- Never invent new crew members. These 7 are canonical.
- The collective noun is "your AI crew" or "the SuperSeller crew."

## Content Formatting

### Headlines
- Lead with the benefit or hook
- 8 words or fewer for social, 12 or fewer for blog
- Use title case for blog headings, sentence case for social

### Body Copy
- Maximum 2-3 sentences per paragraph
- Use line breaks generously in social posts
- Bullet points for features/benefits (keep to 3-5 items)
- Bold key phrases for scannability

### CTAs
- Primary: "Become a Super Seller" (links to https://superseller.agency)
- Secondary: "See [Crew Member] in action" (links to relevant product page)
- Tertiary: "Talk to us on WhatsApp" (for direct engagement)
- Never: "Learn more", "Click here", "Submit" (generic and weak)
