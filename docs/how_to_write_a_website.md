Ryan Deiss–Style Website Playbook

A technical, comprehensive, step-by-step tutorial on structuring, laying out, and writing website content the way DigitalMarketer teaches.

⸻

0) Core principles (Deiss/DigitalMarketer)
	1.	Everything maps to the Customer Value Journey (CVJ). Your pages and CTAs must intentionally move visitors through: Aware → Engage → Subscribe → Convert → Excite → Ascend → Advocate → Promote. Don’t place “buy now” CTAs on pages meant to Engage; sequence matters.  ￼
	2.	Optimize customer value, not pages in isolation. Offers and page types ladder up to a single value system (Lead Magnet → Tripwire → Core Offer → Profit Maximizer → Return Path). Your site IA should reflect this funnel architecture.  ￼
	3.	Homepage is a system hub, not a brochure. It routes traffic to the right next step for each intent segment using a proven, section-by-section formula.  ￼

⸻

1) Discovery & Planning (S-01 … S-07)

S-01. Define the CVJ map. For each stage, list: primary offer, proof asset, CTA, and success metric. (Example: Engage → “Problem-solving blog post” → CTA: “Get the checklist” → Metric: time on page, scroll depth.)  ￼
S-02. Catalogue offers using CVO. Identify at least one Lead Magnet, optional Tripwire, Core Offer, and Profit Maximizer. Map each to pages you will build.  ￼
S-03. Message hierarchy. For your core audience, write:
	•	Who it’s for → Problem → Why previous attempts failed → Your mechanism/approach → Specific outcome → CTA. (Deiss teaches starting copy by explicitly naming the audience and problem.)  ￼
S-04. Sitemap by journey stage. Minimum set:
	•	Engage: Blog Hub, 3–5 pillar posts
	•	Subscribe: Lead-magnet Landing Page + TY page
	•	Convert: Sales/Service page(s), Checkout/Booking
	•	Excite/Ascend: Onboarding hub, Upsell page
	•	Advocate/Promote: Case studies, Reviews/Referrals page  ￼
S-05. KPI plan. Assign a single macro-goal per page (e.g., homepage → segmentation click; sales page → purchase).
S-06. Wireframe quickly. Produce low-fi wireframes for each template before writing copy. (Use any standard wireframe kit to expedite.)  ￼
S-07. Proof inventory. Gather testimonials, logos, numbers, demos, FAQs—allocate each proof element to a page section.

⸻

2) Homepage Blueprint (section-by-section)

Goal: Route each visitor to the right next step (segment + intent), not to say everything to everyone. This mirrors Deiss’s “high-converting homepage” workshop formula.  ￼

H-01. Above-the-Fold (hero) = Promise + Primary CTA + Secondary CTA
	•	Headline: Outcome for [ICP] without [common obstacle].
	•	Subhead: Mechanism in one line.
	•	Primary CTA (journey-aligned): Get the [Lead Magnet] or Start Free Audit.
	•	Secondary CTA: See Plans (for ready buyers).

H-02. Problem → Mechanism → Outcome strip (3 blocks)
	•	You’ve tried X. It fails because Y.
	•	Our approach: one-paragraph mechanism.
	•	Outcome: specific end state + time frame.

H-03. Segmented “Choose Your Path”
	•	3–5 cards for your main use cases or roles; each routes to its own landing/sales page (Convert) or pillar content (Engage).

H-04. Credibility bar
	•	Client logos, ratings, “as seen in”, certifications; link to full case studies.

H-05. Lead magnet module (Subscribe)
	•	Single form module with bullets: what they get, when, format. (This is your CVJ Subscribe step embedded.)  ￼

H-06. Offer overview (skimmable)
	•	3-step how-it-works; pricing teaser; button to pricing/sales page.

H-07. Results & Proof
	•	Outcome cards with metrics, 1–2 video testimonials, 1 case study.

H-08. Content sampler (Engage)
	•	3 featured pillars mapped to top pains; each has a content-upgrade CTA.

H-09. Risk reversal + FAQ
	•	Objection-first FAQs; money-back or guarantee statement (if applicable).

H-10. Footer (utility + trust)
	•	Deep nav, compliance links, contact, social, mini-opt-in.

⸻

3) Sales / Service Page Template (Convert)

SLS-01. Headline = Specific Outcome + ICP + Time/Mechanism.
SLS-02. Problem narrative (Why usual fixes fail).
SLS-03. Introduce mechanism (your unique method).
SLS-04. Proof stack (case studies, numbers, screenshots).
SLS-05. Offer components (what’s included, deliverables, timelines).
SLS-06. Pricing & plans (visual tier table if applicable).
SLS-07. Risk reversal (guarantee, terms).
SLS-08. FAQ ordered by objection priority.
SLS-09. CTA + alternate CTA (book call, start trial).

This structure aligns to DM’s direct-response approach and “12-step” copy frameworks used across their workshops/packs.  ￼

⸻

4) Lead-Magnet Landing Page (Subscribe)

LM-01. One-screen promise. Headline states the transformation.
LM-02. 3 bullets outcome-focused.
LM-03. Form (name + email only).
LM-04. Trust micro-proof: logos, small testimonial.
LM-05. TY page → “next step” offer. This bridges from Subscribe to Convert in the CVJ.  ￼

⸻

5) Blog System (Engage → Subscribe)

BL-01. Pillar architecture: 3–5 cornerstone guides addressing primary pains; each has an embedded content upgrade.
BL-02. Post template (editorial): hook, promise, teaching, proof, next step. DigitalMarketer has long taught structured blog planning and conversion-minded content.  ￼
BL-03. Category pages promote opt-ins aligned to the topic cluster.
BL-04. Internal links: always route to the correct stage offer (don’t over-CTA).  ￼

⸻

6) About, Case Studies, Contact (supporting templates)
	•	About: audience-first (who we help/why it’s hard/our approach), values, leadership proof, CTA to discovery call.
	•	Case Study: client → problem → method → quantified results → lesson → CTA.
	•	Contact/Booking: friction-free form, calendar embed, expectation copy, trust signals.

⸻

7) Copywriting Rules (Deiss-style, practical)
	1.	Lead with fit. First lines specify who it’s for to self-qualify.  ￼
	2.	Problem → Why it persists → Mechanism → Outcome → CTA. Keep this arc inside every section, not just the page.
	3.	Proof early and often. Each scroll segment pairs a claim with evidence.
	4.	One goal per page; one primary CTA per viewport.
	5.	Sequence, not slogans. Every CTA maps to a CVJ stage; don’t jump stages.  ￼

⸻

8) Information Architecture (IA) by Journey Stage

CVJ Stage	Page Types	Primary CTAs	Success Metric
Aware/Engage	Blog hub, pillars, comparison content	Read more, Get checklist	Scroll depth, time on page
Subscribe	Lead-magnet LP, newsletter	Download / Join	Opt-in rate
Convert	Sales page, Pricing, Demo/Booking	Buy / Book a call	Order/booking rate
Excite	Onboarding hub/portal	Complete step 1	Completion rate
Ascend	Upsell/Expansion pages	Add-on / Upgrade	Attach rate, ARPU
Advocate/Promote	Reviews, Referral page	Leave review / Refer	Reviews per customer, referrals


⸻

9) Wireframing & Layout Process (practical)

W-01. Wireframe homepage and one template per journey stage (Engage, Subscribe, Convert) using a low-fi kit; validate flow before design.  ￼
W-02. Annotate each section with: purpose (CVJ stage), key message, proof asset, CTA.
W-03. Run a “routing test”: from the hero, can each major segment reach its next step in ≤2 clicks?
W-04. Only after copy is approved, move to hi-fi comps.

⸻

10) Writing Each Section (fill-in frameworks)

Hero (Homepage/Sales)
	•	Headline: [ICP] get [Outcome] without [Obstacle] in [Timeframe/Mechanism].
	•	Subhead: One-sentence mechanism.
	•	Buttons: Primary (journey step), Secondary (alternate intent).

Problem/Mistake Section
	•	Lead: “If you’re [ICP], you’ve likely tried X… it fails because Y.”
	•	Bridge: “We use [Mechanism] so you get [Outcome].”

Proof Section
	•	Mini-case: “In [N weeks], [Client] saw [Result metric].” Link to full case study.

Offer Section
	•	What’s included, how it works, when it happens, what you’ll get. Tie each item to a desired outcome, not a feature list.

FAQ Section
	•	Write objections as questions, ordered by likelihood to block purchase. Answer with proof or policy.

(These patterns reflect the direct-response approach DM applies across pages, emails, and VSLs.)  ￼

⸻

11) Content System to Feed the Site (operational)
	•	Editorial planner: choose 3–5 pillar topics, then 6–8 supporting posts per pillar; all posts include a content-upgrade opt-in.  ￼
	•	Post structure: clear promise, scannable subheads, examples, internal links to related posts and the matching Lead Magnet.  ￼
	•	Distribution: email your list and drive retargeting from content to conversion offers. (DigitalMarketer has long tied blog → list growth → monetization.)  ￼

⸻

12) On-Page SEO & Conversion Hygiene
	•	One primary intent keyword + semantic variants per page.
	•	Title tag ≤60 chars; meta description 140–160 chars with CTA.
	•	H1 matches search intent; H2s follow the teaching sequence.
	•	Images: descriptive filenames + alt text; compress.
	•	Place 1 primary CTA per viewport; secondary CTA is visually subordinate.
	•	Implement scroll-depth and event tracking for each CTA.

⸻

13) QA & Launch Checklist
	•	Every page mapped to a CVJ stage; no orphan CTAs.  ￼
	•	Homepage routes each key segment in ≤2 clicks.  ￼
	•	Each claim paired with proof.
	•	Forms validated; thank-you pages contain the next-step offer.
	•	Mobile readability tested (40–60 char line length, 16px min).
	•	Page speed: <2.5s LCP on 4G.
	•	Analytics goals set per page’s macro-goal.
